Many critics of God use suffering and evil on earth to attack faith in God. They claim an all-loving and powerful God would not allow tragedy and suffering to exist. The assertion is that if God can't stop evil, he is therefore weak, and if he won't stop evil, he is cruel. This is a 3rd grade level philosophical argument that misunderstands the concept of the title, Divine Providence. As the Catechism of the Catholic Church states in CCC 302, creation has goodness and adjusted perfection, but it did NOT spring from God's hands complete. Humanity was created in a state of journeying towards ultimate perfection that hadn't been attained. God guides us through his blueprint where free will and physical laws work together under him. 

This argument becomes more futile when we apply logic to how our minds as humans utilize freedom. Saint Thomas Aquinas beat this argument by showing how God's omnipotence is made more potent by his ability to bring good out of bad. God doesn't cause evil-human beings do as they abuse their gift of free will to reject goodness. God permits this however because he respects the freedom he gave us, and wants us to find him on our own and not be robots. His providence is so very powerful that he can bring good out of the worst things that can happen into an ultimate design of good. If God deleted all evil, he would delete all love and free will at the same time, reducing us to mindless robots. 

Saint Augustine of Hippo showed that our complaints about suffering are nothing more than complaints of a blind guy looking at a nice painting two inches away. From our limited, natural finite perspective, a tragedy looks like an error. But God is entirely infinite and outside of our limited time minds, he views all of this from beginning to end in an eternal glance. He sees how something today will forge on of us into a saint ten to twenty years from now, or echo to save thousands. To ask God to show us his entire plan to our limited brains is like asking a chess grandmaster to explain a strategy to a newborn. Just because you can't see the infinite and ultimate purpose through a tragedy doesn't mean that purpose doesn't exist, it just means our minds are finite, which they are. 

Finally, the proof of the Divine Providence is found hanging at the cross at calvary. The execution of Jesus-his torture and murder, as he was the innocent Son of God-was the greatest moral crime and evil in the history of the universe. Yet, through Gods design, he used that to accomplish the greatest good ever, eternal salvation and our redemption and freedom from sin. Frank Sheed noted that providence means God doesn't promise us a life with no problems or bad things, but a salvation in and through the problems. We don't worship a chaotic universe that is ruled by randomness and chance, and our God isn't indifferent to us. We worship the wise, timeless, and King of the universe.
