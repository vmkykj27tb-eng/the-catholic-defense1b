While human reason can look at creation and deduce that an omnipotent, intelligent creator exists, reason alone has strict limits. It can prove and show us that an all powerful God exists, but it doesn't tell us who he is on a personal level, what he is like, and what his plan is for us. To solve this, God did not leave us in the dark to solve who he is, he gave us the ability to have faith in him and truly know him by coming into the world as Jesus Christ. This act is simply known as "Divine Revelation" within the Church. Through this revelation, God invites humanity into a true personal relationship, making known truths that completely transcend what human logic could ever discover on our own understanding.


The Catholic Church distinguishes between two different ways God appeared to the world in Natural Revelation and Divine Revelation. Natural Revelation is when God speaks to us silently, now whether that is order, beauty, or the scientific laws of the world. This is truth that we all as humanity can see by just looking outside and using reason and brain power. Divine Revelation is different, however, as Divine Revelation is when God directly speaks to us. God began this slowly in the Old Testament, making the covenants with Abraham, Moses, and all the other prophets, giving them messages, laws, and promises to pass to the people of Israel.


Catholic theology teaches that these processes of divine communication reached its peak and completion in God's son, Jesus Christ. Jesus is not just a prophet bringing a message from God like the many before him, he  is the GOD HIMSELF WRAPPED IN HUMAN FLESH. Therefore, Christ is the perfect and ultimate revelation of the Father. Because Christ revelated everything necessary for our salvation, the Catholic Church shows that Public Revelation is closed. No new doctrine, books in the Bible, or prophecies that will be added to change Christs message two thousand years ago.


The ultimate debate is not whether God revealed himself, but how the revelation is handed down to us without getting corrupted. Christ didn't bring us the Bible, instead he entrusted his words to the apostles. They gave it to their apostles and so on through Apostolic Succession with their preaching (Sacred Tradition) and their inspired writing (Sacred Scripture). To ensure that the truth could never be distorted or twisted improperly over the centuries, Christ established the Catholic Church which gave us the authoritative guide in the Magisterium to protect and interpret his revelation for everybody over all ages of humanity.
