As sad as it is, in the modern world, many people think that faith and reason cannot coexist. The truth is far from that, actually, as the Church teaches that true faith is indeed deeply logical, and that our intelligence comes straight from God. God designed us specifically to be able to seek the truth, so that we could find him on our own, and not just be robots. To defend this claim, the Catholic Church uses an idea introduced by Saint Pope John Paul II. John Paul II taught that faith and reason are the human spirits two wings to look at truth. For example, can an airplane fly with only one wing? No. Can a bird fly with only one wing? Obviously not. So then if you use that same logic to apply it for ourselves, if you use only one of your wings, that being reason without faith, you become cynical, depressed, and empty. On the other hand, however, if you use the other wing that being faith without reason, you have nothing to backup and defend your faith. 


The Catholic Church officially formalized this teaching during Vatican I. The council declared our reasoning as human beings to be so powerful that it can prove the existence of God by just looking at the world we have. By studying order, design, and laws of physics (like Aquinas) in the universe our minds come to the conclusion there has to be a supreme creator. As the Catechism of the Catholic Church states in CCC 36, "Our holy mother, the Church, holds and teaches that God, the first principle and end of all things, can be known with certainty from the created world by the natural light of human reason."


If reason can indeed prove the existence of God, why do we need faith? The reason why is because our reason has limits as human beings. Reason can tell us God does exist, but it can't tell us who God is. This can get confusing when looking between which religion is correct. One person using their own reason could deduce that God obviously exists, but could believe and think that Judaism is the correct religion, whereas another person could use that same reason to believe in Anglicanism, for example. Reason alone couldn't discover the mystery of the Trinity, incarnation, or God's plan through Jesus Christ. Faith is our "yes" to the reasoning of God existing through the Church and Scripture. 


In conclusion, because God created science and faith, both can never really contradict. If there is ever a conflict between a science and Catholic doctrine, it means we as humans have misunderstood the science, or misinterpreted the Catholic doctrine. True science and true faith always point back to one thing, the creator of the universe, the Trinity, and the Catholic Church.
