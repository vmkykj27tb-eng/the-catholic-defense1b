Many people, even Christians deny the Resurrection of Christ as a fairytale saying no man could rise from the dead. Many argue that the early church just lied about the tomb to rise a movement. This just is a failure of historical knowledge. The claim that the Apostles started a lie goes against psychology and history. The Apostles died in the worst ways, why would someone for example (Peter) get hung on a cross upside down for a lie? People will die for something they believe to be true, but no one would suffer horribly for something they made up. Every Apostle except for John was tortured and killed in horrible ways, and they refused to take back their faith in the risen Christ. 

So this argument collapses when the Minimal Facts criteria is used. First, Jesus was definitely executed by a Roman crucifixion. Second, the Apostles and followers of His went from hiding to preaching the Gospel. Third Saul of Tarsus (Paul) went from one of the main catalysts of Christian persecution to converting after seeing Christ. Fourth, the tomb was empty when checked. Roman and Jewish authorities, seeing this large movement opposing them claiming that Christ rose could've crushed it by just producing Christ's body. They couldn't because He rose.

The Catechism of the Catholic Church states in CCC 639 that the mystery of Christ's resurrection was historically verified and witnesses. The New Testament isn't false, the earliest writings of it 1 Corinthians 15, were written within a few years of the crucifixion, listing 500 eyewitnesses who saw Christ risen. Saint Augustine argued that if there was no resurrection, then the explosive conversion of millions in the Roman Empire by a handful of "uneducated" fisherman, carpenters, and others without any political or financial backing would be more impossible then the resurrection itself.

The theological triumph of the early church and Christianity is based on the resurrection. Thomas Aquinas said the wounds Christ retained in his body weren't weakness, but trophies of the greatest sacrifice in human history. Jesus is King and his resurrection proves that.
