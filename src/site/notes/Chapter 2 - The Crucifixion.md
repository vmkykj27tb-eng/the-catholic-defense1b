After denying the life of Christ happened, deniers pivot back to a desperate, false theory called the Swoon theory. The claim is that Jesus didn't die on the cross, but merely of exhaustion and was buried in a state of shock from the difficulties. It also says that he was revived by his apostles because the tomb was cooler to trick His followers into believing in a resurrection. This is a medical impossibility in the time of late Roman Empire, and within ancient Roman protocol. The Roman Empire didn't fumble execution, they were so good at it for their time. To say a man could survive a full, hard Roman crucifixion, break out of a guarded tomb, and convince thousands he was the Lord is just impossible. 

We have to now look at the forensics of Roman executions. Before Christ even was crucified, he was subjected to flagrum flagellation, he was whipped with iron balls and sheep bones. Modern forensics confirms this would've induced shock, dropped blood pressure, and forced fluid to his lungs. To breath on a cross they had to dig their hands and feet into nails to get air. 

The Catechism of the Catholic Church outlines the sacrifice with precision in CCC 614, stating His sacrifice on the cross is unique. The sacrifice surpasses all other sacrifices. This wasn't a failure by the Romans, it was a historical death by crucifixion. The proof is the eyewitnesses and verified by modern medicine. When the Roman solider Saint Longinus drove a spear into the side of Christ to just make sure he was dead, a large amount of "blood and water" came out (John 19:34). Medically, this is a pleural effusion, proof that the heart had ruptured and that Christ was dead. 

Saint Thomas Aquinas wrote that Christ suffered every form of pain so that His death would be the anchor for salvation. The executioners, under pain of their own suffering, pronounced His death, and His body was wrapped in burial spices before being sealed behind limestone and guarded. Frank Sheed talked about how the cross wasn't a defeat, but where sin was ultimately paid for. Christ isn't just a bruised man, He is God who resurrected from crucifixion.
