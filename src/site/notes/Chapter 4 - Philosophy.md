Many skeptics of the Church throughout history including now have always said that faith is just blind, emotional, incompatible with reason and science. This however is a theological blunder, faith doesn't eliminate reason, it works and relies on it. True theology needs objective fact philosophy because you can't defend God's existence without the reason and the natural laws of reality. Philosophy is not a threat to our faith, it is the shield against Atheism and all other religions.

To defeat these claims, however, there is need to know the laws of logic. The first, and in my opinion the best rule to use is the Law of Non-Contradiction: something simply can't be both 1 and 
"not 1" at the same time in the same way. If someone says "objective truth can't exist," the statement defeats itself. If their statement happens to be true, then objective truth does exist, shattering their argument. Objective truth just can't contradict objective truth.

Many Atheist, Anti-Theist, and Secular debaters and skeptics use broken logical fallacies to attack our doctrines and miracles instead of using facts. The most common on of these broken logical fallacies is the Straw Man fallacy, when someone just exaggerates or lies about one of our traditions just to knock it down or make it seem illegitimate. An example is many people (not atheists mainly) will claim that we as Catholics worship statues or pagan idols. That is a complete and utter lie. They will retreat into that argument even if it is a straw man and fallacy because they don't want to look at the Catechism or the actual history/facts. 

Ultimately philosophy is our perfect pair with theology. When Saint Thomas Aquinas built his defense of the Catholic faith, he didn't rely on just faith and emotion, he used logical tools of philosophers like Aristotle to prove reality and objective truth. By understanding the rules of logic and correct thinking, Catholic defense becomes bullet-proof, as everything in the Magisterium is indeed true.
