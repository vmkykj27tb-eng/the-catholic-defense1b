Secular and Non-Christians always like to hate on the Trinity and mock it, claiming it is Pagan, or just an absurdity that makes no sense and violates logic. They commit a strawman everytime by saying, "do you guys worship one God or three gods." This however is sheer ignorance, as even (for example) some of the best Muslim debaters I've watched don't even use Trinitarian arguments because they know the argument is garbage. As the Catechism of the Catholic Church states in CCC 253, the Trinity is One. We don't worship three gods but One in three persons. The Church does NOT teach that one God equals three gods, nor that one person is three people. We confess that God is one in nature (what he is) and three in person (who he is). One human nature is only possessed by one human person, but infinite Divine Nature (God) is posssessed by three co-eternal persons. (The Father, Son, and Holy Spirit)


If you have some confusion this could help. We have to apply the rules of philosophy to attributes of God. He is love. Love can't exist seperately from others, love needs a lover (The Father) a Beloved (Christ) and a bond between them (The Holy Spirit). Frank Sheed used this to prove that if God was solitary he couldn't be love by nature, because he would have nothing and nobody to love. He would have been dependent on creating the universe for his love, which means we would've been who he depended on, which violates his characteristic of not needing anything. The Trinity helps with this, from all of eternity the Father is the infinite lover, the Son is perfectly Beloved, and the infinite bond of that relationship is the Holy Spirit.


People also like to use the fact that the exact word "Trinity" is not in the text of the Bible. Saint Thomas Aquinas had an excellent rebuttal for this by distinguishing between the word and its meaning. The absence of a term does not mean absence of truth of the meaning. Trinity as the word was coined by the early church to summarize the meaning, as it was the best word to use, but the Trinity is woven into scripture. At Christs Baptism, the entire Trinity is present (Matthew 3:16-17). In Matthew 28:19, Jesus explicitly commands his Apostles to baptize using a singular name of three distinct things: "in the name of the Father, and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit." If the Son and the Spirit were created and lesser, baptizing people in that formula alongside God the Father would've been blasphemous and idolatry. 

To bridge this using another Doctor of the Church, Saint Augustine of Hippo illustrated that the Trinity is in our human soul. He argued that man is created in the image and likeness of God, our minds reflect the Trinitarian nature of the Creator. A human mind has three distinct inseparable parts: our memory, our intellect, and our will. The memory stores thought, intellect understands thought, and will loves and acts upon that thought. They aren't separate, but rather three distinct parts working in unity. If a finite being like us can possess this, it is illogical to think that the Creator of the universe cannot.
